## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are Korean beginner and intermediate recreational tennis players choosing a first racket, replacing an ill-fitting racket, or trying to understand equipment without specialist knowledge. Secondary users are growth-minded club players and equipment enthusiasts who compare model years, specifications, feel, prices, and availability. Their core job is to understand, compare, receive a trustworthy recommendation, and continue to a real purchase path for rackets and compatible strings.

### Brand Personality

Minimal, premium, and trustworthy. The product should feel editorial and expert-led while explaining technical equipment in plain Korean. It earns confidence through consistent data, visible sources, and honest tradeoffs instead of hype. The immutable visual reference is `https://racketlab-omega.vercel.app/`.

### Aesthetic Direction

Preserve the Omega version's spacious, image-led, white/gray presentation with a restrained dark hero, crisp typography, and the racket as the visual focal point. Use the existing lime brand accent sparingly for orientation and action. Avoid decorative gradients, generic dashboard cards, excessive color, fake precision, and visual changes that make the canonical build drift from Omega. The experience must work on mobile and desktop, including keyboard focus, reduced-motion preferences, and readable contrast.

### Design Principles

1. One fact, one source: the same racket specification and five-axis score must appear everywhere.
2. Explain before selling: translate technical specifications into useful Korean guidance and disclose tradeoffs.
3. Complete every journey: each visible navigation item and CTA must reach a real, useful destination.
4. Commerce without false claims: show only verifiable pricing and offers; use clear fallback states when no seller is registered.
5. Preserve the Omega signature: improve consistency, accessibility, and completion without redesigning the approved visual identity.
